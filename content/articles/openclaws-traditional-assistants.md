                <p class="lead">Understanding the fundamental differences between OpenClaw's agentic approach and traditional AI assistants.</p>
                
                <h2>Fundamental Differences</h2>
                <p><strong>Traditional Assistants:</strong> Reactive, session-scoped, tool-limited</p>
                <p><strong>OpenClaw:</strong> Proactive, persistent, tool-integrated</p>
                
                <h2>Capability Comparison</h2>
                <ul>
                    <li><strong>Memory:</strong> Traditional assistants forget between sessions; OpenClaw maintains persistent context</li>
                    <li><strong>Action:</strong> Traditional assistants can only suggest; OpenClaw executes real tasks</li>
                    <li><strong>Coordination:</strong> Traditional assistants work alone; OpenClaw coordinates multi-agent swarms</li>
                    <li><strong>Learning:</strong> Traditional assistants don't learn; OpenClaw compounds insights over time</li>
                </ul>
                
                <h2>When to Use Each</h2>
                <p>Traditional assistants remain useful for simple queries and one-off tasks. OpenClaw excels at complex, multi-step operations requiring coordination and persistence.</p>
